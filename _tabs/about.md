## About Me

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Agronomy (Crop Science) at the Federal University of Lavras (UFLA), Brazil, with research focused on **Precision and Digital Agriculture**.

My work connects **agronomy**, **data science**, and **remote sensing** to solve field-scale problems using practical and reproducible workflows.

In 2024, I spent 12 months as a Visiting Researcher at the **University of Georgia (UGA)**, collaborating with interdisciplinary teams in agronomy, remote sensing, and agricultural data science.

## Research Interests

- Machine learning for crop prediction
- Deep learning and computer vision from UAV imagery
- Satellite-based vegetation indices and geospatial analytics
- Crop maturity and yield modeling
- Variable-rate technology and drone spraying applications
- Practical AI systems for agriculture

## Technical Stack

**Python:** scikit-learn, PyTorch, pandas, Ultralytics, Jupyter

**R:** tidymodels, caret, ggplot2

**Geospatial & Imaging:** QGIS, Pix4D, Google Earth Engine

**Workflow & Collaboration:** Git, GitHub, Google Colab

## Research Philosophy

I am especially interested in building solutions that move beyond experimental plots and can be translated into **usable tools for growers, researchers, and field technicians**.

That means combining model performance with interpretability, scalability, and operational practicality.
